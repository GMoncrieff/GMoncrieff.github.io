// About

I combine algorithms with biodiversity data to solve conservation problems. Most of my work uses machine learning and satellite imagery to measure and monitor the natural world, from invasive plants in the fynbos to land cover change across whole regions.

I am a spatial data scientist in the Global Science team at The Nature Conservancy, and I am affiliated with SAEON's Fynbos Node and SEEC at the University of Cape Town. I have spent more than fifteen years building data and machine learning systems across research, industry, and government. I live in Cape Town, and when the screen time gets too much I head for the mountains or the sea.
